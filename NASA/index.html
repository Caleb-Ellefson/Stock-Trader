  • Addition of graphene in the titanium fibers and main structures of the ship, rover and components, like the batteries, to add more strength without increasing its mass, protecting our energy system. for comparison, Graphene and Niobium has a melting point over 3000 °C , parker solar probe, a mission to explore the sun, is made from sheets of Titanium-Zirconium-Molybdenum, an alloy of molybdenum, with a melting point of about 4,260 F (2,349 C).
  • Capacity of niobium and graphene in batteries and resistance to recharge time, at high temperatures like no other technology
  • Use Multi-Mission Radioisotope Thermoelectric Generator (MMRTG) to convert heat from the natural decay of radioisotope materials into electricity. RPSs are ideal for space missions because they are compact, durable and reliable, providing continuous power over long periods of time, in view of light absorption due to dense clouds of venus, solar energy would be unfeasible and the MMRTG operated well on other NASA missions such as Mars.
  • Using a Stirling Cooling System to keep the rover's temperature below 200°C (Stirling model)
